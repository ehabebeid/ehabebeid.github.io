Temple of Jupiter Capitolinus

Arguably the most important temple in Ancient Rome, the Temple of Jupiter Optimus Maximus (Temple of Jupiter Best and Greatest) was located on the souther portion of the Capitoline Hill; its remains are currently beneath Palazzo dei Conservatori and Palazzo Caffarelli (Krautheimer 12). Its surroundings consisted of a sacred precint by the name of area Capitolina which included temples and shrines dedicated to other deities commonly associated with Jupiter (Coarelli 42-44).

The Temple was rebuilt three times after fires that consumed it. The earliest iteration of the building dates back to the sixth century BC and is considered an example of Etruscan architecture. It likely had a rectangular plan, and was by far the largest temple in the region (Claridge 229-235).
