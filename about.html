About the Project

The Capitoline Hill in Rome is a palimpsest of urban design and function. Its history stretches from antiquity to the modern priod, and its uses include the religious and the secular, the governmental and the cultural. To each of these functions, in different eras, the Capitoline was—and often is—of utmost importance.

This website is a digital guide consisting of a partial history of the Hill, with a focus on its most important buildings, how they relate, build on, and respond to each other. Through it I hope to render more legible part of the site's complicated and storied history.

Should you have any questions, comments or suggestions, please contact me at ee253@cornell.edu.
